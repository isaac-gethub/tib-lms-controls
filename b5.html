Section 1 · The Degree-of-Engagement Spectrum

The first question to answer on any engagement is: how hands-on are we? The source session describes a spectrum from light-touch advising at one end — assessing and recommending without directly designing or testing — through to fully embedded delivery at the other, where the controls team is deeply involved in design, validation, testing, and implementation across all phases.

Degree-of-Engagement Spectrum
Advisory / AssessDesign & ValidateTest & Implement

• Assess client's controls environment
• Identify gaps and opportunities

• Deliverable: Assessment report

• Validate with business process owners

Hands-On End
• Test DEF and OEF

• Confirm controls work as designed
• Primary relationship: Test manager
• Deliverable: Test results + evidence
💡
Why This Matters Before Day One
Understanding where you sit on the spectrum before an engagement begins shapes everything — what your client expects, what your team is accountable for, what success looks like, and which stakeholders you will spend most of your time with. Misaligned expectations on degree of engagement are one of the most common sources of client friction on large programmes.

Section 2 · The Four Primary Engagement Types

The source session identifies four primary engagement types. Each has a distinct scope, a distinct set of deliverables, and a distinct primary stakeholder relationship. Recognising which type you are on — and potentially which combination — is the first step to setting up the engagement for success.

1
Assessment & Recommendation
Advisory
The practitioner reviews the client's current risk and controls environment — their existing framework, SAP configuration, process design — and provides an independent assessment of gaps and opportunities. The primary deliverable is a findings and recommendations report. This is valuable as a standalone engagement or as a scoping exercise before a larger programme.
Scope
Current controls environment review
Deliverable
Findings and recommendations report
Key Relationship
Internal Audit / Compliance function
Timing
Standalone or pre-transformation scoping
2
S/4HANA Transformation
Full Lifecycle
The controls team is embedded from the beginning of the programme, working through all four phases: solution confirmation → design and build → testing → go-live. Activities shift in each phase: risk assessment and preliminary RCM drafting in solution confirmation; controls design and business validation in design and build; operating effectiveness testing in the testing phase. The primary deliverable — the Risk and Controls Matrix (RCM) — is progressively refined from a preliminary draft at solution confirmation to a fully validated, tested document by go-live.
Scope
Full transformation lifecycle — all four phases
Deliverable
Progressively refined RCM: preliminary → validated → tested
Key Relationship
Programme manager · Functional leads · System integrator
Timing
Solution Confirmation → Design & Build → Testing → Go-Live
3
Controls Testing
DEF + OEF
The client already has a controls framework in place. The controls workstream joins specifically to test whether controls are designed effectively (DEF) and operating effectively (OEF). The key activity is working with the programme's testing team to identify which test scripts are relevant from a controls perspective, then reviewing system testing results to confirm controls work as designed. This is common during SIT and UAT phases.
Scope
Existing controls framework — test DEF and OEF
Deliverable
Test results · Evidence packages · Gap findings
Key Relationship
Testing lead · Programme test manager
Timing
System integration testing (SIT) · UAT phase
4
Remediation
Time-Sensitive
Time is of essence. Clients who have received significant deficiency or material weakness findings from their external auditor need the controls team to overhaul the affected areas before the next audit cycle. The scope is defined by the findings. The practitioner is working against a deadline, with documented audit findings that define exactly what must be addressed — and a hard stop at the next external audit.
Scope
Deficiency / material weakness areas from external auditor
Deliverable
Remediated controls · Re-test evidence · Audit-ready packages
Key Relationship
External auditor · CFO · Audit committee
Timing
Post-audit-finding — before the next audit cycle

Section 3 · Stakeholders, Integration & The Practitioner's Frame

Understanding your engagement type does more than clarify your scope — it tells you who your most important stakeholder is. In an assessment engagement, it is Internal Audit or the Compliance function. In a transformation engagement, it is the programme manager and the functional leads. In a testing engagement, it is the test manager. In a remediation engagement, it is the external auditor and the CFO.

⚠️ Field Insight — Controls-Security Integration Point
The source session explicitly flags this: practitioners should connect the dots between how security roles are designed and how those roles affect the risk and controls framework. A role that creates a SoD conflict is not just a security issue — it is a financial risk. A control that relies on restricted system access must be coordinated with the security team. The security workstream designs SAP user roles and authorisation objects. The controls workstream identifies SoD conflicts in that role design and maps them to the financial risk framework. These two workstreams are not independent — failure to coordinate them is one of the most common delivery failures on large S/4HANA programmes.

The Four Engagement Types — Summary
Type 01
Assessment & Recommendation
Review current state, identify gaps, recommend improvements. Deliverable: findings report. Key relationship: Internal Audit. Advisory mode — no direct design or testing.
Type 02
S/4HANA Transformation
Embedded from start. Four phases: solution confirmation → design and build → testing → go-live. Deliverable: progressively refined RCM. Key relationship: programme manager / functional leads / SI.
Type 03
Controls Testing
Client has controls framework — test DEF and OEF. Work with testing team to identify control-relevant test scripts. Deliverable: test results and evidence. Key relationship: test manager.
Type 04
Remediation
Time-sensitive. Scope defined by external auditor findings. Overhaul affected areas before next audit cycle. Deliverable: audit-ready remediation evidence. Key relationship: external auditor / CFO.
Key Definitions
TermDegree of Engagement
The spectrum from light-touch advisory (assess and recommend only) to fully embedded hands-on delivery (design, validate, test, implement). Determines scope, deliverables, and primary stakeholder relationships. Must be agreed before the engagement begins to avoid client misalignment.
TermDesign Effectiveness (DEF)
A test conclusion that confirms a control is suitably designed to mitigate the identified risk — the right control is in place for the right risk. DEF testing happens before operating effectiveness testing and is a prerequisite for OEF reliance. A control that fails DEF cannot be relied upon for OEF.
TermOperating Effectiveness (OEF)
A test conclusion that confirms a control has been operating as designed throughout the test period — not just that it is in place. OEF testing requires evidence that the control was performed consistently, completely, and by a competent person (for ITDMs and manual controls) or that the system enforced it on every transaction (for automated controls).
TermRisk and Controls Matrix (RCM)
The primary deliverable of a controls workstream. Each row maps a risk to its category, inherent rating, control, test procedure, and residual rating. In a transformation engagement, the RCM begins as a preliminary draft at solution confirmation and is progressively refined — with business validation, design updates, and test results added — through to go-live.
TermControls-Security Integration
The coordination required between the controls workstream and the security and roles design workstream. SoD conflicts are simultaneously a security issue (role design) and a financial risk (controls framework). The two workstreams must align on role design, access decisions, and SoD conflict remediation to avoid gaps and contradictions in the final go-live environment.
